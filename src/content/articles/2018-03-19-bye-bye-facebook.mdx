>Both <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/03/17/us/politics/cambridge-analytica-trump-campaign.html">the Times</a> and <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/news/2018/mar/17/cambridge-analytica-facebook-influence-us-election">the Guardian</a> describe this as a _“data breach”_, but I don’t think that’s entirely descriptive of what went on here. When I hear _“data breach”_, I think that a password got stolen or a system was hacked into. But Facebook VP Andrew Bosworth tweeted that there was nothing that was stolen — users willingly gave their information to an app, which went behind their backs to use the information in a somewhat sketchy way that users did not expect.<br /><br />Facebook is more than happy to collect the world’s information, but it is clear to me that they have no intention for taking full responsibility for what that entails.**<a href="https://pxlnv.com/linklog/cambridge-analytica-facebook/">Fifty Million Facebook Profiles Harvested for Cambridge Analytica</a>**

>The data that Facebook leaked to Cambridge Analytica is the same data Facebook retains on everyone and sells targeting services around. The problem is not shady Russian researchers; it’s Facebook’s core business model of _collect, store, analyze, exploit_.**<a href="https://twitter.com/Pinboard/status/975013825010458624">Maciej Cegłowski</a>**

I just deleted my Facebook account. I've never been particularly opinionated about privacy but I can't continue supporting <a href="/omfacebook">their business model</a>.
